Rawon (Javanese: ꦫꦮꦺꦴꦤ꧀) is a typical Indonesian dish originating from Ponorogo, East Java, which is a black meat soup with a special spice mixture that uses kluwek. This food is more than 1,000 years old. Rawon from Ponorogo spread throughout East Java and is known as a typical East Javanese dish.

The meat for rawon is generally beef cut into small pieces, mainly the brisket. However, sometimes rawon also comes with chicken. The soup seasoning is very typical of Indonesia, namely a mixture of shallots, garlic, galangal (laos), coriander, candlenuts, lemongrass, turmeric, chili, kluwek, salt, and vegetable oil. All of these ingredients are ground, then sauteed until fragrant. This spice mixture is then put into the meat broth together with the meat. The typical dark color of rawon comes from kluwek, this is because in Ponorogo culture it is still thick with magical things. Outside Indonesia, rawon is called Black Soup.

Rawon disajikan bersama nasi, dilengkapi dengan tauge berekor pendek, telur asin, daun bawang, kerupuk udang, daging sapi goreng (empal), dan sambal. Beberapa pelengkap lain yang juga dapat dihidangkan bersama rawon adalah olahan jeroan. Di Pasuruan, rawon juga lazim dikonsumsi dengan mendol (perkedel tempe) dan disajikan di atas daun pisang. Kunci lezat rawon juga berada pada olahan kuahnya yang berasal dari kaldu daging yang digunakan, baik dari lemak daging, tulang muda, maupun kulit.
